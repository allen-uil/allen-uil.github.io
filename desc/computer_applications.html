You remember BIM class from middle school? That class alongside some studying might make you a great fit for this competition. With Microsoft Office ('13, '16, or '19 typically) at your side, constestants have to complete as many of a series of tasks as possible. This may include populating spreadsheet with data to make a pie chart, importing the data into Access to file a report, and creating a memo to share your boss the pie chart you just created. Either way, this contest is admittingly divide and conquer: doing as much as you know ASAP and then worrying about the rest.

  • A packet with Word, Excel, and Access instructions (not necessarily in that order)
  • 38 minutes in sections
    • 5 minutes at the start for a typing tiebreaker
    • 3 minutes once the packet is given to review it and make notes
    • 30 minutes to actually do the test, with printing having to be completed within this time limit
  • Scoring is done according to a rubric
  • Each competitor must have their own laptop to use, as well as a printer to print off their results
